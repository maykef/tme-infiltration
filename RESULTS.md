## 1. Datasets used (after filtering)

- **Pretraining — Jackson & Fischer 2020 (breast IMC):** 100 slide graphs, 285,851 cells, **33 markers** (45 channels minus 7 Ru counterstain, 2 DNA intercalators, 3 all-NaN `exprs` channels). Self-supervised, no outcome labels.
- **Fine-tune + eval — Moldoveanu 2022 (melanoma ICI IMC):** 30 slide graphs = 30 patients (one slide each), **14 responders / 16 non-responders**, **35 markers** (38 channels minus 3 technical). 112,497 cells.
- Graphs: k-NN (k=12) in (x,y), undirected; node features arcsinh + per-dataset z-scoring (panels never pooled). Evaluation is **leave-one-patient-out** (30 folds).

## 2. Headline result — GNN vs. Giuliani pair-correlation baseline

| Model | AUROC (LOPO) |
|---|---|
| **Spatial-graph GNN** (pretrained trunk + fine-tune) | **0.460  (95% CI [0.253, 0.679])** |
| **Giuliani baseline** — cross-PCF g(macro.mono ~ Tc.ae) @ r=10.5 µm | **0.833  (95% CI [0.660, 0.964])** |

**GNN does NOT beat baseline.** The hand-crafted spatial statistic (macrophage/monocyte ↔ activated CD8⁺ T-cell co-localization at 10.5 µm) clearly separates responders from non-responders, while the learned GNN sits near chance at this sample size. This is reported plainly: at N=30 with cross-panel (breast→melanoma) transfer, the GNN does not beat the targeted biological feature.
